1. Whitepapers (5–15 pages, technical, solution‑oriented)

"Nexus Joint Standard (NJS) v1.0: A Modular Interoperability Protocol for Humanoid Robotics"

-Abstract – Overview of the need for a common joint standard.

-Problem statement – Proprietary connectors and incompatible joint designs hamper modularity.

-Proposed standard – Mechanical, electrical, and data interface specifications.

-Case studies – How NJS would benefit the Pleasure, Domestic, and Guardian units.

-Validation – CAD simulations, prototype joint tests, torque limits.

-Conclusion – Roadmap for industry adoption



"Skin Emulation for Social Robots: Material Properties, Tactile Sensing, and Maintenance"

-Problem – Existing silicone/TPE skins lack durability, realistic texture, or heat dissipation.

-Material survey – TPE, silicone, self‑healing polymers.

-Proposed layered architecture – Structural base, heating/cooling channels, skin layer.

-Testing results – Grip friction, tear resistance, thermal regulation.

-Future research – Haptic feedback, wound self‑repair.



Thesis Excerpts (pages from a master’s or PhD thesis, adapted for RDH)
Example C: "Real‑time Emotion Recognition for Companion Robots Using Multi‑modal Sensor Fusion"
Introduction – Why robots need to read human emotional cues.

-Methods – Fusing camera (facial expressions), microphone (tone), and wrist sensors (GSR).

-Dataset – Collected from 50 participants interacting with a robot prototype.

-Results – 92% accuracy in distinguishing joy, sadness, frustration, contentment.

-Implications – Improving companionship and elder care.



"Passive Cooling Channels for High‑Density Actuator Arrays in Humanoid Torsos"

-Motivation – Prevent skin surface from exceeding safe temperature (45°C) during prolonged use.

-Design – Integrated micro‑channels in the carbon‑fibre frame.

-CFD simulations – Heat dissipation vs. actuator power.

-Prototype build – 3D printed torso with active/passive cooling.

-Conclusion – Channel geometry that reduces peak temperature by 18°C.



Research Summaries (short, accessible overviews – 1–2 pages)
"Survey of Open‑Source Walking Gaits for Bipedal Robots"
Lists existing gait libraries (e.g., from Robotis OP2, NAO, Unitree).

-Compares stability, energy efficiency, and ease of implementation.

-Recommends a baseline gait that RDH builders can start from.



"Comparison of Low‑Cost Force Sensors for Robotic Fingertips"
Tests 5 sensors under $20 (resistive, capacitive, piezoresistive).

-Measures sensitivity, linearity, drift, and durability.

-Provides a decision matrix for builders choosing fingertip sensors.


"State of the Art in Artificial Muscles (2026) – A Builder’s Guide"

-Summarises recent advances in pneumatic, hydraulic, and electro‑active polymer actuators.

-Lists performance metrics (force, contraction speed, cycle life).

-Gives plain‑language recommendations for small vs. large joints.
